ROBIN JOINS FC GOA

Panaji: On Saturday, Robin Singh's social networking account was buzzing quite unusually."Exciting times ahead. The beast is on the move," Robin posted, teasing fans and egging them to guess his next move.Out of action for almost seven months with a knee injury, the India striker is indeed starting a new journey, motivated to begin all over again with Indian Super League finalists FC Goa.

Robin signed a one-year deal with FC Goa on Saturday, making him the biggest and most prominent signing for the Goan franchise so far. Robin was the star striker for Delhi Dynamos in last year's ISL but was forced to miss out on the I-League after injuring himself at the South Asian Football Federation (SAFF) Cup in Trivandrum. Since then, all that Robin has done is work steadfastly towards recoveringfrom his ACL injury, and although it has taken him seven months to get back in shape, he is hungrier than ever before.

"I am feeling motivated and hungry to start all over again. It's no secret that I had an ACL injury and I had to undergo an operation. But now I am raring to go," said Robin, speaking exclusively to TOI.

Caster Semenya runs world leading 800m in Monaco

South Africa's Caster Semenya coasted to victory in the 800m at the Monaco Diamond League in a world leading and personal best time on Friday. Dutchwoman Dafne Schippers won the women's 100m while there was a surprise triumph in the men's 1500m as Kenya's Ronald Kwemoi beat Olympic champions Taoufik Makhloufi, Mo Farah and Asbel Kiprop. The ever-reliable Scholes turned the clock back as he treated the crowd to deft touches, dribbles and sublime passes.

PREMIER FUSTAL

There wasn’t a rare moment in the match when the Bengaluru 5’s captain didn't have the ball. Egged on by a vociferous crowd, who chanted his name throughout the Bengaluru 5’s-Kolkata 5’s match, the Red Devils legend gave his teammates and opponents a lesson on how one can control the game by keeping possession of the ball.
