e all know that presentation is the
most important thing when it comes to
cakes. But move over fondants — mir-
ror-glaze cakes are the new trend-setters in the
world of cakes. After Russian confectioner Olga
Noskova broke the internet with her extrava-
gant mirror-glaze cakes, the trend has become a
buzzword across patisseries in the country.
The term mirror-shine cakes, as they are also
called, implies that the surface of the cake is so
crystal-clear and smooth that you can literally
see your reflection on it. It is made by pouring a
liberal dose of shiny glaze over the cake, with
some pouring off, resulting in an even coat.
Confectioner Karishma Chandani explains
the process: “The glaze has to be made and
poured over a chilled cake. The glaze or the
sugar syrup has to be boiled at the right tem-
perature to prevent it from getting very thick.
Also, it has to be of a particular consistency so
it coats the full cake when poured. It needs to be
strained to prevent air bubbles and blended
very well. The gelatin in the glaze gives it a mir-
ror-like shine. These cakes have a great aesthet-
ic appeal, but health freaks
avoid them as a lot of colour and gelatin are
used to make them.”
Also, these cakes are not light on the pocket as
a kilogram costs around
`
2,500. Moreover, a lot of
care is required to make these cakes. “Because
the glaze is made of sugar and gelatin, utmost
care is needed to not sweeten the base beyond a
certain point. Instant glaze is also available in
the market, but it tastes bad,” says baker Shreya
Khemka Ladia.
Baker Shripriya Gupta adds, “This new trend
in the pastry world is very attractive as it is all
about a beautiful, shiny glaze. But it involves a
lot of technique. It is a temperamental product,
which is sensitive to temperature. Also, even
after it is complete, it needs to be handled very
carefully.”
However, bakers point out that the requests for
these cakes are on the rise.
“I have made a few for peo-
ple. I feel it has to do with
the fact that these are seen
as not cakes, but more like
entremets. You can give the
same glaze to mousse and
cheesecakes too,” says
baker Priya Vijan Gupta.

MEGA, the luxury Swiss watch brand, has
been a leader in the field of watchmaking
since its inception in 1848. With precision at
its core, the brand has also earned the
trust of the world’s best athletes
through its role as the ‘Official
Timekeeper of the Olympics
Games’, a position it has fulfilled on
26 occasions since 1932.
With the brand now poised to con-
tinue its long tradition of timekeeping
at Rio 2016, it celebrated the upcoming occasion at
a special event in Bengaluru with one of the
world’s top badminton players — Saina Nehwal.
As a gesture of good luck, PH Narayanan, Brand
Manager, OMEGA India, presented Saina
Nehwal with special watches.
Commenting on her association with the
brand, Saina said, “There is a natural connec-
tion with OMEGA as it stands for precision,
excellence and accuracy which are also the cor-
nerstones of every sport. I am honoured to be
here today for a brand that has been timing the
greatest sporting event for the last 84 years.”
As a tribute to the Rio 2016 Olympic Games,
the brand launched three timepieces which are
inspired by the glory, triumph and unbreak-
able unity that brings the world together — THE
SEAMASTER DIVER 300M RIO 2016, THE SEA-
MASTER BULLHEAD RIO 2016 and THE SPEED-
MASTER MARK II RIO 2016.
